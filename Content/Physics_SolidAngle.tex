 Solid Angle (rotational measurement of spherical direction):\footnote{$[\Omega]=[sr]$ Steradiant, "Raumwinkel"}
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
$
\Omega = 
\frac{A}{R^2} = 
\int\limits_A \frac{\cos(\varepsilon)}{R^2} \, dA	\qquad	\in \left[ 0, 4\pi \right]
$
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
where $A \equiv$ spherical Area, $R \equiv$ spherical Radius (radial distance), and \newline
$\varepsilon = d\vec{A} \cdot \vec{e}_n = \angle(dA,R)$ inclination angle of $dA$ to $R$ along the curved surface $A$
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
$
dA = \frac{R^2}{\cos(\varepsilon)} \sin(\theta) d\theta d\varphi = \frac{R^2}{\cos(\varepsilon)}d\Omega
$
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
where $\varphi \equiv$ horizontal angle, $\theta \equiv$ vertical angle
\vspace{2ex} \newline

Symmetry of wave propagation from electromagnetic radiation in vacuum (Reversibility of Beam according \textbf{Reversal Principle}):\footnote{Umkehrbarkeit des Strahlenganges der geometrischen Optik}
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
$
d\Omega_g = 
\frac{\mathscr{M}}{\mathscr{J}} dA_g =
\frac{dA_b}{R} \frac{\cos(\varepsilon_b)}{R} = 
\frac{dA_b \cos(\varepsilon_b)}{R^2}
$
\newline
$
d\Omega_b =  
\frac{\mathscr{M}}{\mathscr{J}} dA_b =
\frac{dA_g}{R} \frac{\cos(\varepsilon_g)}{R} =  
\frac{dA_g \cos(\varepsilon_g)}{R^2}
$
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
$
d\Omega = 
\sin(\theta) d\theta d\varphi = 
\frac{dA_{\perp}}{R^2} = 
\frac{\cos(\varepsilon)}{R^2}dA = 
\mathscr{J} \cdot d\Omega_g = \mathscr{M} \cdot dA_g =
\mathscr{J} \cdot \mathscr{M}_b \cdot \frac{\varepsilon_b}{R^2}
$
\vspace{2ex} \newline 
where 
$\mathscr{J} \equiv$ Flux Density,
$\mathscr{M} \equiv$ Radiant Exitance
